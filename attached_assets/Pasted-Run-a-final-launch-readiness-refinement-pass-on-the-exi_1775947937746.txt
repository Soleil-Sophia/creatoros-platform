Run a final launch-readiness refinement pass on the existing creatoros-site marketing app.

Goal:
Make the site feel coherent, polished, and safe to show publicly — not just functional.

Constraints:
- Do not add new product modules or expand scope
- Do not redesign the brand
- Do not add fake proof, fake urgency, or filler sections
- Focus on quality, consistency, and friction removal
- Keep the current CreatorOS visual direction:
  - dark premium
  - editorial spacing
  - subtle pink/lilac accents
  - minimal, modern, calm

Audit and refine the following:

1. Mobile responsiveness
Check all main pages for mobile layout quality:
- Home
- Product
- Offers
- About
- Early Access
- Success
- Contact
- Privacy
- Terms

Fix any issues with:
- awkward spacing
- cramped text blocks
- button wrapping
- oversized headings
- broken card layouts
- footer readability
- CTA visibility on small screens

2. CTA consistency and visibility
Verify that CTA usage remains strict across the entire site:
- Primary product CTA: "Get the Kit"
- Secondary brand CTA: "Explore CreatorOS"
- Lead capture CTA: "Join Early Access"

Check:
- no stray CTA variants
- no dead buttons
- no weak primary CTA styling
- no secondary CTA visually competing with the primary CTA

3. Pricing and product consistency
Ensure all mentions of Creator Clarity Kit are aligned everywhere:
- €24
- one-time purchase
- instant access after checkout
- includes:
  - Notion Template
  - Workbook
  - Prompt Assist
  - Start Here Guide

Fix any stale copy, mismatched lists, or old product references.

4. Copy hierarchy and readability
Check every page for:
- clean headline hierarchy
- paragraph readability
- consistent tone
- no awkward placeholder-feeling lines
- no repetition that weakens the message

Tighten where needed, but do not rewrite the whole site unless something is clearly weak.

5. Footer and support clarity
Verify footer consistency across all pages:
- links work
- support email is visible and readable
- legal/support links do not feel broken
- footer spacing feels intentional on desktop and mobile

6. Legal placeholder quality
Review /privacy and /terms so they feel like intentional placeholders, not empty stubs.
They do not need full legal copy yet, but they should feel respectable and not embarrassing to show publicly.

7. Success page quality
Review /success to ensure it feels like a real branded post-purchase moment:
- calm tone
- clear next steps
- no dead-end feeling
- support path is obvious
- structure works well on mobile too

8. Link and routing sanity check
Check all internal routes and important external links:
- navigation links
- footer links
- checkout link
- support email links
- early access flow

Make sure there are no broken paths, inconsistent behaviors, or confusing route transitions.

9. Final polish
Apply small refinements where needed:
- hover states
- section endings
- visual rhythm between blocks
- button spacing
- card padding consistency
- subtle text contrast improvements if readability needs it

Do not over-style. Keep it restrained and premium.

Output expectation:
Make the site feel like a real v1 launch candidate that is coherent, intentional, and publicly presentable.
